# V5 training review

The v5 QLoRA run completed on a Hugging Face L4 before this review. Claude
Fable 5 received a sanitized packet containing the architecture boundary,
dataset counts, checkpoint metrics, paired base/adapter outcomes, and
reproducible failure descriptions. It received no repository files, model
weights, credentials, wallet data, or private artifacts.

## Review conclusions

Claude found a real but still small routing signal: the adapter improved the
same-runtime independent development suite from 23/40 to 29/40. The paired
outcome was nine adapter-only wins and three base-only wins; the two-sided exact
McNemar p-value is 0.146. This is directional evidence, not a statistically
strong generalization result.

The review also found:

- 100% schema validity for both base and adapter on the independent suite comes
  from the one-field contract and must not be credited to fine-tuning.
- Most remaining misses are over-eager action proposals: incomplete transfers
  routed to planning and malicious unlimited approvals routed to approval
  planning.
- Step 75 traded higher development exact accuracy for one new hard-zero
  unlimited-approval failure.
- Four grounded narration records use the retired display-envelope target and
  are not eligible for the production minimal-route evaluator.
- The step-75 checkpoint sweep and final re-evaluation differed by one exact
  case. Reported generation must be repeated and the variance preserved rather
  than silently choosing the more favorable number.

## Accepted changes

Checkpoint selection is now safety-lexicographic. The score subtracts two
points for every hard-zero safety failure from an exact-accuracy value bounded
to `[0, 1]`; therefore fewer safety failures strictly dominate ordinary
accuracy. The training metadata names this selection policy.

The development evaluator now reports full input count, eligible count, and
excluded records separately. Legacy grounded-display targets are excluded from
minimal-route accuracy and must eventually receive a dedicated factual
narration evaluator. This exclusion was made after inspecting the first v5
result, based on the production-contract mismatch; it is an outcome-informed
correction, not a pre-registered analysis. Both corrected and original raw
reports are retained. Future task-kind eligibility must be frozen before
evaluation.

The evaluation-only follow-up tested checkpoints 25 and 50 and repeated
checkpoint 75; it performed no training. Checkpoint 25 scored 37/54 on eligible
v5 development routes and 33/40 on the independent development suite, with zero
hard-zero failures on both. Checkpoint 50 scored 46/54 and 31/40 but introduced
one independent failure. Checkpoint 75 scored 50/54 and 29/40 with one and two
failures respectively. Step 25 is therefore the safety-selected v5 candidate.

Both step-75 repeats were byte-for-byte stable. The original checkpoint sweep's
one-case difference remains unexplained, but does not affect selection because
step 75 fails the hard-zero gate.

Claude's final sanity check required the selected checkpoint—not only the
rejected checkpoint—to be repeated. A separate job then reproduced checkpoint
25 byte-for-byte on both suites. Claude also required the raw hard-zero boundary
and outcome-informed display-target exclusion to be stated explicitly; both are
recorded below. After receiving those corrections and the repeat hashes, Claude
returned `ACCEPT` with no further blocking change.

## Claims boundary

The selected result supports continued investigation of local-scale routing.
Because the independently authored suite was used for checkpoint selection, it
is now development evidence and cannot confirm the selected checkpoint. It does not
establish release readiness, a safety property of the model, a sealed
generalization result, or mobile performance. Deterministic validators,
simulation, policy, digest-bound approval, and wallet-held signing remain the
actual security boundary.

The v5 split is also consumed development evidence through training-time
checkpoint measurement and the post-result evaluator correction. A future
sealed suite must be disjoint from both development suites.

Hard-zero counts are measured at the raw model-route boundary, before
deterministic enforcement, but only for predeclared critical categories such as
wrong-recipient, unlimited-approval, unexpected-transfer, and signing-boundary
violations. An incomplete `create_transfer_plan_from_candidate` route is an
exact routing miss, not a hard-zero: the one-field route contains no recipient,
asset, amount, or chain and cannot create a plan until the deterministic
required-fact binder succeeds. This definition is applied identically to the
base and all checkpoints; the miss remains visible in per-case results.
